The SDF $m(1+r_{f})=1-\mathbb{S}\mathbb{R}[r_{m}]z_{m}$ prices the return of the total endowment, $r_{m}$, and the risk-free rate, $r_{f}$, where $z_{m}=(r_{m}-\mathbb{E}[r_{m}])/\mathbb{S}[r_{m}]$. Suppose that the stock's dividends grow at an IID rate of $g$. For some constant $b>0$, conjecture the price $P(D)=bD$: 
\begin{equation}
	P(D)=\mathbb{E}[m((1+g)D+P((1+g)D))]\:\Rightarrow\:b=-1-(1+r_{f})\alpha_{g}^{-1}
\end{equation}
where 
\begin{align}
	\beta_{g}&=\mathbb{C}[g,r_{m}]/\mathbb{V}[r_{m}]\\
	\alpha_{g}&=\mathbb{E}[g-r_{f}]-\beta_{g}\mathbb{E}[r_{M}-r_{f}]
\end{align}
