\noindent On 2020-03-31, PFE traded for 33.42 USD. In one month, PFE can either go up to 36.76 USD or down to 30.08 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 32.15 USD call. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{c}$ is 0.31, $B_{c}$ is 9.32 USD, and so $C_{0}$ is 1.04 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.31 shares of PFE and long 9.32 USD in the risk-free asset.
