\noindent On 2020-03-31, NKE traded for 89.38 USD. In one month, NKE can either go up to 98.32 USD or down to 80.44 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 93.48 USD call. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{c}$ is 0.73, $B_{c}$ is 58.66 USD, and so $C_{0}$ is 6.59 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.73 shares of NKE and long 58.66 USD in the risk-free asset.
