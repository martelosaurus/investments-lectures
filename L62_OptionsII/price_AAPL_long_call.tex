\noindent On 2020-03-31, AAPL traded for 273.36 USD. In one month, AAPL can either go up to 300.7 USD or down to 246.02 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 264.99 USD call. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{c}$ is 0.35, $B_{c}$ is 86.02 USD, and so $C_{0}$ is 9.66 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.35 shares of AAPL and long 86.02 USD in the risk-free asset.
