\noindent On 2020-03-31, NKE traded for 89.38 USD. In one month, NKE can either go up to 98.32 USD or down to 80.44 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went short a 84.24 USD call. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{c}$ is 0.21, $B_{c}$ is 16.88 USD, and so $C_{0}$ is 1.89 USD. To hedge your position, you should go long 0.21 shares of NKE and short 16.88 USD in the risk-free asset.
