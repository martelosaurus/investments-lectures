\noindent On 2020-03-31, DIS traded for 117.65 USD. In one month, DIS can either go up to 129.42 USD or down to 105.88 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 128.87 USD put. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{p}$ is 0.02, $B_{p}$ is 2.59 USD, and so $P_{0}$ is 0.24 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.02 shares of DIS and long 2.59 USD in the risk-free asset.
