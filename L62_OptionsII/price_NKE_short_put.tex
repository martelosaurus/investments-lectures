\noindent On 2020-03-31, NKE traded for 89.38 USD. In one month, NKE can either go up to 98.32 USD or down to 80.44 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went short a 84.24 USD put. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{p}$ is 0.79, $B_{p}$ is 77.6 USD, and so $P_{0}$ is 6.99 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.79 shares of NKE and long 77.6 USD in the risk-free asset.
