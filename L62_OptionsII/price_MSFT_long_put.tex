\noindent On 2020-03-31, MSFT traded for 162.01 USD. In one month, MSFT can either go up to 178.21 USD or down to 145.81 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 150.95 USD put. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{p}$ is 0.84, $B_{p}$ is 149.55 USD, and so $P_{0}$ is 13.46 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.84 shares of MSFT and long 149.55 USD in the risk-free asset.
