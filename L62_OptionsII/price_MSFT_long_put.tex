\noindent On 2020-03-31, MSFT traded for 162.01 USD. In one month, MSFT can either go up to 178.21 USD or down to 145.81 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 173.15 USD put. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{p}$ is 0.16, $B_{p}$ is 28.49 USD, and so $P_{0}$ is 2.57 USD. To hedge your position, you should go long 0.16 shares of MSFT and short 28.49 USD in the risk-free asset.
