\noindent On 2020-03-31, DIS traded for 117.65 USD. In one month, DIS can either go up to 129.42 USD or down to 105.88 USD. Suppose that on 2020-03-31, you went long a 123.88 USD call. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{c}$ is 0.76, $B_{c}$ is 80.39 USD, and so $C_{0}$ is 9.02 USD. To hedge your position, you should go short 0.76 shares of DIS and long 80.39 USD in the risk-free asset.
