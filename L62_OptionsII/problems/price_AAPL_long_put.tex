\noindent On 2020-04-30, AAPL traded for 254.29 USD. In one month, AAPL can either go up to 279.719 USD or down to 228.861 USD. Suppose that on 2020-04-30, you went long a 234.1718 USD put. What was the option premium that you paid or received? How would you hedge your position?

\vspace{9pt}

\noindent\textbf{Solution.} $\Delta_{p}$ is 0.1044, $B_{p}$ is 29.1805 USD, and so $P_{0}$ is 2.6262 USD. To hedge your position, you should go long 0.1044 shares of AAPL and short 29.1805 USD in the risk-free asset.
